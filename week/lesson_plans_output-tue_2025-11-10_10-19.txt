**Class: 16:00 Stage 7 | Method: Single-Lane (Top-and-Tailing)**
**Focus:** Improving movement sequence, treading water, and butterfly kick technique.
**Swimmers:** 8
**Equipment:** Kickboards, Woggles

**Introduction & Safety (3-5 mins):**
*   Welcome swimmers and briefly outline the lesson objectives.
*   Emphasize poolside safety rules, especially around entry and exit.
*   Quickly check for any swimmer who may have specific needs or concerns for today's session.

**Warm-up (5-7 mins):**
*   **Poolside:** Sit on the poolside, splash water, safe entry.
*   **In Water:**
    *   Walk forwards, backwards, and sideways, focusing on body position.
    *   Push and glide on front and back, practicing a smooth transition.
    *   Use woggles for support and to practice arm action.

**Main Activity (10-12 mins):**
*   **Movement Sequence Refinement (Indiana Marrow):**
    *   Focus on the movement sequence for 1 minute, linking strokes and sculls.
    *   Break down the sequence into smaller parts, focusing on one element at a time (e.g., sculling head first, then feet first).
    *   Use woggles for support to help with rotation and floating.
*   **Treading Water Progression (Ella Harris, Oscar Beck):**
    *   Tread water using an eggbeater action for 30 seconds.
    *   Begin with a smaller duration and build up.
    *   Provide feedback on body position and leg action.
*   **Butterfly Stroke Introduction (Edmund Tucker - Stage 6, mentioned in context of Stage 7 needing practice):**
    *   Focus on the dolphin kick with woggles, emphasizing hip drive and undulation.
    *   Break down the kick into smaller parts (chest press, hip push).
    *   "Dolphin kick" using legs only, working on body motion.

**Contrast Activity (5-7 mins):**
*   **Obstacle Course (Indiana Marrow, Xander Yafai-Flanary):**
    *   Set up a simple obstacle course in the pool using 4-5 objects.
    *   Swimmers must navigate the course with feet off the bottom throughout.
    *   This reinforces skills like body control, propulsion, and changing direction.

**Cooldown & Fun (3-5 mins):**
*   **Fun Game:** "Simon Says" using swimming actions (e.g., "Simon says do 5 arm pulls," "Simon says tread water").
*   Encourage swimmers to discuss what they enjoyed or found challenging.
*   Positive reinforcement for effort and participation.

**Class: 16:30 Stage 2 | Method: Single-Lane (Top-and-Tailing)**
**Focus:** Developing confidence with floats and rotations, improving log roll technique.
**Swimmers:** 6
**Equipment:** Woggles, Floats

**Introduction & Safety (3-5 mins):**
*   Welcome swimmers and their parents/carers.
*   Briefly explain the plan for today's session, focusing on water confidence and fun.
*   Review poolside safety and safe entry into the water.

**Warm-up (5-7 mins):**
*   **Poolside:** Sit on the poolside and splash water, safe entry.
*   **In Water:**
    *   Blow bubbles rhythmically with nose and mouth submerged.
    *   Practice pushing from the wall and gliding on the front and back.
    *   Travel 5 meters on the front and back using a recognised leg action (feet on the pool floor).

**Main Activity (10-12 mins):**
*   **Floating Progression (Alfie Hudspith, Scarlett Watts):**
    *   Focus on moving from a flat floating position on the back and returning to standing without support.
    *   Use woggles for initial support, gradually reducing reliance.
    *   Encourage swimmers to relax and find their balance.
*   **Log Roll Practice (Alfie Hudspith, Isla-Mai Haywood, Mason Curtis, Mila Sosna, Scarlett Watts):**
    *   Focus on log rolls from back to front and front to back.
    *   Start with supported rolls, focusing on head position and body control.
    *   Use woggles to help with buoyancy and body position.
    *   Break down the roll into smaller stages if needed.

**Contrast Activity (5-7 mins):**
*   **Tuck to Rotate (Isla-Mai Haywood, Mason Curtis, Mila Sosna, Scarlett Watts):**
    *   Perform a tuck to rotate from a flat floating position on the front to a back floating position, then return to standing.
    *   This builds on the floating and rotation skills.
    *   Provide visual demonstrations and verbal cues.

**Cooldown & Fun (3-5 mins):**
*   **Game:** "Seahorse Race" using woggles, encouraging movement and control.
*   Practice exiting the water without support.
*   Positive reinforcement for all attempts and progress.

**Class: 17:00 Stage 6 | Method: Single-Lane (Top-and-Tailing)**
**Focus:** Developing breaststroke technique, improving backstroke leg action, and practicing surface dives.
**Swimmers:** 9
**Equipment:** Kickboards, Woggles

**Introduction & Safety (3-5 mins):**
*   Welcome swimmers and outline the session's focus.
*   Quickly review two examples of how to prepare for exercise and why it's important.
*   Reinforce poolside safety and safe entry.

**Warm-up (5-7 mins):**
*   **Poolside:** Sit on poolside, splash water.
*   **In Water:**
    *   Sink, push off on side from the wall, glide, kick, and rotate into Backstroke.
    *   Sink, push off on side from the wall, glide, kick and rotate into Front Crawl.
    *   Swim 10 metres wearing clothes, focusing on maintaining body position.

**Main Activity (10-12 mins):**
*   **Breaststroke Kick Refinement (Edmund Tucker, Rewan Baban):**
    *   Focus on the breaststroke kick action using kickboards.
    *   Emphasis on "flex," pushing water with the inside of the feet, and keeping knees together.
    *   Break down the kick into stages: Lift and Flex, Push and Squeeze.
*   **Backstroke Leg Action Improvement (Rewan Baban):**
    *   Practice backstroke legs with woggles, focusing on a consistent, continuous kick.
    *   Encourage a good body line and keeping hips near the surface.
    *   "Floppy ankles" and "constant kick" drills.
*   **Surface Dive Practice (Alwin Varughese, Benjamin Pawley, Edmund Tucker, Harvey Crack, Jarryd Box, Layan Baban, Molly Mitchell, Rewan Baban, Tadhg Mason, Thomas Chambers):**
    *   Practice the surface dive in shallow water.
    *   Start with a supported dive (e.g., instructor holding ankles) and progress to unassisted.
    *   Focus on body position and a controlled entry.

**Contrast Activity (5-7 mins):**
*   **Swim 10 metres wearing clothes (Alwin Varughese, Benjamin Pawley, Harvey Crack, Jarryd Box, Layan Baban, Molly Mitchell, Rewan Baban, Tadhg Mason):**
    *   This activity reinforces the importance of body control and stroke efficiency when slightly encumbered.
    *   Focus on maintaining a streamline position and effective propulsion.

**Cooldown & Fun (3-5 mins):**
*   **Game:** "Shout and Signal Rescue" drill (Jarryd Box, Layan Baban).
    *   In pairs, one acts as a swimmer in slight difficulty, the other practices a "shout and signal" rescue.
*   Positive reinforcement for effort and skill development.

**Class: 17:30 Stage 5 | Method: Single-Lane (Top-and-Tailing)**
**Focus:** Developing sculling skills, improving treading water, and introducing basic butterfly stroke.
**Swimmers:** 4
**Equipment:** Kickboards, Floats

**Introduction & Safety (3-5 mins):**
*   Welcome swimmers and briefly outline the lesson's goals.
*   Discuss the importance of water safety and demonstrate an action for getting help.
*   Review safe entry into the water.

**Warm-up (5-7 mins):**
*   **Poolside:** Sit on the poolside, splash water.
*   **In Water:**
    *   Perform 3 different shaped jumps into deep water (with appropriate supervision).
    *   Push and glide 10 metres Backstroke.
    *   Push and glide 10 metres Front Crawl.

**Main Activity (10-12 mins):**
*   **Sculling Progression (Audrey Ogden, Jai Winkett, Opal Hartland, Phoebe Hirst):**
    *   Perform a flat stationary scull on the back.
    *   Practice a feet-first sculling action for 5 metres in a flat position on the back.
    *   Focus on smooth, continuous sculling actions.
*   **Treading Water Practice (Audrey Ogden, Jai Winkett, Opal Hartland, Phoebe Hirst):**
    *   Tread water for 30 seconds.
    *   Start with support if needed (e.g., holding a woggle) and progress to unsupported.
    *   Focus on maintaining a stable body position and effective leg action.
*   **Butterfly Stroke Introduction (Opal Hartland, Phoebe Hirst):**
    *   Focus on the dolphin kick on the front, emphasizing body undulation.
    *   Use a woggle for support while practicing the kick.
    *   Break down the undulation into smaller movements (chest press, hip push).

**Contrast Activity (5-7 mins):**
*   **Handstand and Forward Somersault (Opal Hartland, Phoebe Hirst):**
    *   Practice a handstand and hold for a minimum of 3 seconds.
    *   Perform a forward somersault in shallow water.
    *   These activities develop body awareness and control.
